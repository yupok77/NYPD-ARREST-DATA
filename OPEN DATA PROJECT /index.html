NYPD Arrests Data (Historic)

Exploring arrest records across New York City

About the NYPD

The New York City Police Department (NYPD) is the largest municipal police department in the United States. The NYPD works to protect the people of New York City by enforcing laws, preventing crime, and maintaining public safety across the five boroughs.

About the Dataset

The NYPD Arrests Data (Historic) dataset contains information about arrests made by the NYPD. The dataset includes details such as arrest date, borough, offense type, age group, race, gender, and geographic location.

This dataset is important because it helps people better understand crime trends and law enforcement activity in New York City. It can be used to identify patterns, compare boroughs, and analyze different categories of arrests over time.

This website allows users to explore arrest information in an easy and interactive way. Users will be able to filter arrest records and view charts that analyze trends in the dataset.

Website Features

Information Page

View arrest records from the NYC Open Data dataset in an organized card layout.

Users can filter information by borough, offense type, age group, and more.

Analysis Page

Analyze arrest trends using charts and visualizations.

Users will be able to compare arrests between boroughs and explore crime patterns across NYC.
